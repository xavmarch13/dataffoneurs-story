After taking the red pill, Neo wakes up in a strange world of machines and shapes. Different shapes representing different emotions across movies are floating around him, buzzing and crackling as they move. Afraid of the diversity of these strange shapes and their potentially dangerous aspects, he decides to cluster them by form to determine which are harmful to him and which are not. He hopes this will help him understand the dangerous emotions surrounding him. Using the elbow method, he ensures that his classification is meaningful and accurate.

<iframe src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/plot/redpill/cluster_evolution_anger.html" width="100%" height="520" frameborder="0"></iframe>

<iframe src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/plot/redpill/cluster_sizes_anger.html" width="100%" height="520" frameborder="0"></iframe>

The anger emotion shapes did not seem very dangerous, as the clusters could be divided into mostly two categories: a relatively smooth semi-arc (all clusters except cluster 2) and a more abrupt one with a constant increase in anger (cluster 2). Neo observed a similar pattern for the disgust emotion. It predominantly revolved around the same semi-arc shape, differentiated only by the amount of variability alongside it (cluster 2 versus the others).

<iframe src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/plot/redpill/cluster_sizes_sadness.html" width="100%" height="520" frameborder="0"></iframe>

As he approached sadness, Neo sensed something more dangerous. He noticed completely different patterns compared to before: a high-to-low pattern (cluster 1), a low-to-high pattern (clusters 2 and 4), and a flatter one (clusters 0 and 3). He wondered if these clear sadness patterns could influence other variables, such as the reception of a movie (e.g., its rating). The surprise emotion exhibited similar distinct patterns, including a high-to-low pattern (clusters 3 and 4). However, the majority of movies demonstrated a wave-like pattern (cluster 0).


<iframe src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/plot/redpill/cluster_evolution_surprise.html" width="100%" height="520" frameborder="0"></iframe>

<iframe src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/plot/redpill/cluster_sizes_surprise.html" width="100%" height="520" frameborder="0"></iframe>


Almost reaching the end of his analysis, Neo came across yet another interesting distinction. In most movies, fear seemed to drop toward the end, but with highly varied timings for the peak of fear. Sometimes the peak appeared at the beginning (cluster 5), in the middle (clusters 0 and 6), or at the end (clusters 2 and 3). Neo pondered what impact this might have on other movie variables.

<iframe src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/plot/redpill/cluster_evolution_fear.html" width="100%" height="520" frameborder="0"></iframe>

<iframe src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/plot/redpill/cluster_sizes_fear.html" width="100%" height="520" frameborder="0"></iframe>

Finally, joy presented a pattern unlike any Neo had seen so far. It was mostly high at the beginning, low in the middle, and high again at the end. This could represent the fact that most movies tend to have happy endings (cluster 0). Other patterns included the already-seen low-to-high pattern (cluster 2) and high-to-low pattern (cluster 3).

<iframe src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/plot/redpill/cluster_evolution_joy.html" width="100%" height="520" frameborder="0"></iframe>

<iframe src="{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/plot/redpill/cluster_sizes_joy.html" width="100%" height="520" frameborder="0"></iframe>

Satisfied with his observations and insights, Neo decided to return to the Matrix. There, he hoped to use his newfound understanding of emotions to navigate the complexities of the digital and emotional worlds.
